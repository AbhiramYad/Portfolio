1. CCTV

Closed-circuit television (CCTV), also known as video surveillance,[1][2] is the use of closed-circuit television cameras to transmit a signal to a specific place on a limited set of monitors. It differs from broadcast television in that the signal is not openly transmitted, though it may employ point-to-point, point-to-multipoint (P2MP), or mesh wired or wireless links. Even though almost all video cameras fit this definition, the term is most often applied to those used for surveillance in areas that require additional security or ongoing monitoring (videotelephony is seldom called "CCTV".

2. Fire extingusiers

Fire extinguishers are essential safety tools for schools, and for them to be effective, they must be the right type for different hazards, strategically placed, regularly inspected, and staff/students must be trained to use them. Common types include ABC (for general use, flammable liquids, and electrical fires), water mist (for sensitive equipment), and water (for solid materials).

Art and craft are creative activities involving making things with one's hands, often with the goal of expression or creating something functional or decorative. While the terms are often used together, "craft" generally focuses on skill to achieve a specific, pre-determined outcome, while "art" is more about open-ended expression, process, and emotional impact.
